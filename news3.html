NEWS

Best Overall: Oculus Rift

As the first PC-powered headset to hit the market, the Rift offers a growing library of games and apps, along with an easy setup, ergonomic Touch Controllers, and a surprisingly light and comfortable design. Priced at $399, the Oculus Rift is more affordable than the $499 HTC Vive.

Best Value: Pansonite 3D VR Glasses

Want to explore VR without spending tons of cash? The Pansonite is our value pick, as it supports both Android phones and iPhones, has integrated earbuds and works with most Google Cardboard apps. It's also one of the few VR headsets that also support augmented-reality apps. Best of all, it's less than $50 on Amazon.

Best Mobile VR Headset: Samsung Gear VR

The Samsung Gear VR continues to be the gold standard for mobile VR. The addition of the controller adds a tactile experience to certain games that can't be achieved with a traditional gamepad. The ever-growing library is a cornucopia of new games and apps that have to be experienced to be believed, and you can access it all via voice commands.And there are other applications of the system too.

Latest News and Updates (January 2019)

We just went hands-on with HTC's upcoming Vive Pro Eye, which features integrated eye-tracking. We just received the Vive Wireless Adapter, a peripheral that cuts the cord on the VR headset. Stay tuned for the review.Oculus just announced Oculus Quest, the company's first standalone high-end headset that promises room-tracking and Rift-quality graphics.
